## Macro & Monetary Environment

### Federal Reserve Stance
The Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC) held the federal funds rate unchanged at **3.50%–3.75%** at its April 29 meeting — the third consecutive hold — and market pricing reflects a near-certainty (≈98%) of another hold at the upcoming June meeting. What has changed materially is the rate-cut narrative: earlier hopes for 2026 easing have evaporated.

### Inflation Picture
Inflation has re-accelerated meaningfully:
- **Headline PCE (YoY): 3.5%** — up from 2.8% in February, well above the Fed's 2% target
- **Core PCE (YoY): 3.2%** — stubbornly persistent
- Energy prices have been the primary driver, amplified by the Iran-related oil supply shock (see Geopolitical section)

### Rate Outlook
Bank of America now projects **no rate cuts through all of 2026**, pushing the first reduction to July–September 2027. Markets are pricing roughly a 30% probability of a **rate hike** by December if inflation doesn't moderate. This represents the most hawkish pivot in Fed expectations in over a year and is a headwind for high-multiple growth stocks.

### Key Economic Events This Week
- **Today (June 3):** Broadcom Q2 FY2026 earnings (post-close) — a critical read on AI capex demand
- **Friday, June 6:** May Non-Farm Payrolls — pivotal for Fed re-assessment; strong jobs data would reinforce the "hold longer" narrative
- **Watch:** Any new developments on US-Iran ceasefire talks, which directly impact oil price trajectory

## Geopolitical Factors

### US–Iran Conflict: The Dominant Risk

The most consequential macro wildcard is the active US-Iran military conflict that escalated in early March 2026. US and Israeli military actions against Iran triggered immediate supply chain disruption across global energy markets. Brent crude briefly **exceeded $110/barrel** — a multi-year high — and approached **$117/barrel** in peak sessions.

The Strait of Hormuz, through which approximately **20% of the world's daily oil shipments** transit, became the flashpoint. Any sustained closure or even the credible threat of disruption drives an outsized energy price premium. The global natural gas market has been similarly affected, with LNG availability tightening and price volatility spiking.

**Current Status:** US-Iran negotiations are ongoing, and Tuesday's market action showed investors were cautiously optimistic — the major indexes rallied modestly despite the backdrop. However, the situation remains fragile and represents the largest tail risk to the current bull market.

**Market Impact:**
- Energy stocks and defense contractors are outperforming
- J.P. Morgan's medium-term Brent crude target remains ~$60/bbl, suggesting current levels reflect a geopolitical risk premium that should compress on resolution
- Economists warn of **stagflation risk** if high oil prices persist alongside slowing growth

### Trade Policy
US tariff and trade policy remains a secondary risk factor, though it has been overshadowed by the Iran situation. Investors should monitor any escalation in US-China trade friction, particularly around semiconductor export controls which directly impact NVIDIA and TSMC.

## Sector Deep Dives

### Artificial Intelligence

The AI infrastructure trade is in full acceleration. The hyperscalers' collective $725 billion capex commitment for 2026 — up 77% from last year's $410 billion — provides extraordinary revenue visibility for the picks-and-shovels layer:
- **Amazon:** $200 billion
- **Microsoft:** ~$190 billion
- **Alphabet:** $175–$185 billion
- **Meta:** $115–$135 billion

**NVIDIA (NVDA):** Paradoxically underperforming its fundamentals in 2026, up only ~15% year to date despite posting 85% revenue growth year over year in its most recent quarter. Management guided next quarter at 96% revenue growth YoY — an extraordinary acceleration for a company at this scale. The market appears to be rotating into second-order AI beneficiaries. Still the unambiguous GPU datacenter monopolist.

**Meta Platforms (META):** Down ~4% YTD despite 33% revenue growth, driven by AI-enhanced ad impressions and conversions. Sentiment received a boost this week after NVIDIA CEO Jensen Huang publicly praised Meta's AI progress, lifting shares roughly 1% intraday on June 2.

**Marvell Technology (MRVL):** The session's standout mover. Shares closed at $290.79, surging **+32.52%** on Tuesday after Jensen Huang declared Marvell the next "trillion-dollar company" — validation of Marvell's custom silicon and AI networking interconnect strategy. The company's Q1 FY2027 revenue hit a record $2.418 billion (+28% YoY), with Q2 guidance of $2.7 billion (+35% YoY). New product: the **Teralynx T100** — a 102.4 Tbps AI/cloud switch chip claiming 25% lower power consumption and leading latency metrics.

**Key Competitive Dynamics:** The emerging battleground is **custom AI silicon (ASICs)** vs. NVIDIA's general-purpose GPUs. Google TPUs, Amazon Trainium/Inferentia, Microsoft Maia, and Meta MTIA are all gaining traction. Broadcom and Marvell are the primary beneficiaries as silicon foundry partners for these custom programs.

### Hardware & Semiconductors

**TSMC (TSM):** The essential backbone of the entire AI semiconductor ecosystem. Q1 FY2026 results showed revenue +35.1% YoY and net income +58.3% YoY. Management upgraded full-year 2026 revenue guidance to **above 30% growth**, anchored by AI high-performance computing demand. TSMC is now **raising 3nm pricing by up to 15%** in H2 2026, with full adoption from NVIDIA, AMD, Google, AWS, and cloud providers — a rare demonstration of pricing power. At ~24x forward earnings, TSMC trades at a discount to Broadcom and NVIDIA despite superior growth and profitability metrics. Bernstein has a $430 price target; BofA maintains Buy.

**Broadcom (AVGO):** Trading ~$327/share, +41% YTD. The company reports **Q2 FY2026 earnings after the close today** — the day's biggest event. Consensus expects:
- Revenue: ~$22 billion (+47% YoY)
- AI semiconductor revenue: **~$10.7 billion (+140% YoY)** — this would be the largest single-quarter AI revenue number in the company's history
- Adjusted EBITDA margin: ~68%
- EPS: ~$2.40

Broadcom's moat lies in its **custom ASIC programs** (XPUs) for hyperscaler AI workloads and its dominant AI networking portfolio (Jericho routing chips, Tomahawk switching). If results beat expectations, expect a significant after-hours move.

**AMD (Advanced Micro Devices):** Ramping next-generation **EPYC "Venice"** server processors on TSMC's 2nm process. AMD remains a credible challenger to Intel in data center CPUs and is gaining share in AI inference workloads with its MI-series GPUs. Consensus analyst price targets imply ~39% upside.

**ASML / Applied Materials (AMAT):** Semiconductor equipment names continue to benefit from the global fab buildout. Applied Materials trades at a forward P/E of ~26.56x, below the semiconductor equipment peer group average of ~34.5x — an anomaly for a company growing at double-digit rates.

**Oracle (ORCL):** Gained 8–16% in the recent software rally. The company's cloud infrastructure (OCI) is gaining traction as a third hyperscaler alternative, particularly for AI training workloads where it competes aggressively on pricing.

**Salesforce (CRM):** The laggard of the group, slipping 0.8% Thursday while peers rallied. Investors appear skeptical about Agentforce monetization timelines. Worth watching — if AI agent revenue inflects, the stock has significant upside from current levels.

### 5. Applied Materials (AMAT)
**Rating: Buy**

**Thesis:** Applied Materials is the most attractively valued play on the multi-decade semiconductor equipment upgrade cycle. As TSMC, Intel, Samsung, and new government-funded fabs ramp advanced node production globally, Applied Materials' deposition, etch, and inspection tools are mandatory purchases. The stock trades at a notable discount to peers.

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Forward P/E | ~26.5x |
| Peer Group Avg. Forward P/E | ~34.5x |
| YoY Performance | +56.3% |
| Discount to Peer Group | ~23% |

**Competitive Moat:** Near-irreplaceable expertise in thin-film deposition and materials engineering. Deep customer integration — equipment is co-developed with leading foundries for each new process node. High aftermarket services revenue creates recurring income stream.

**Key Risk:** Semiconductor equipment cycles are notoriously volatile. A capex pause by foundries — which has happened in 2019 and 2023 — could cause orders to slow sharply. US export controls to China also cap a portion of the addressable market.

**Rating: Buy**

**Thesis:** Micron is the most deeply undervalued large-cap semiconductor name. At a forward P/E of 12x, the market is pricing in peak-cycle concerns, but the AI-driven memory supercycle — particularly high-bandwidth memory (HBM) for AI training and inference — is still in its early innings. Every major AI GPU (NVIDIA H200/B200, AMD MI series) requires growing HBM content per chip.

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Forward P/E | ~12.2x |
| Peer Group Avg. | ~17x |
| YoY Performance | +240.6% |
| Premium/Discount | ~30% discount to peers |

**Competitive Moat:** One of only three companies globally (alongside Samsung and SK Hynix) capable of manufacturing leading-edge DRAM and NAND. HBM market position is improving with HBM3E ramp.

**Key Risk:** Memory is a commodity business subject to severe pricing cycles. If AI chip shipments slow or NVIDIA encounters export restrictions, HBM demand could cool faster than expected. Competition from Samsung and SK Hynix on HBM3E is intense.

## Risk Factors

Ranked by near-term severity:

1. **🔴 Iran-US Conflict Escalation (HIGH):** A breakdown in ceasefire talks or new military action could spike Brent crude above $120/barrel, re-igniting inflation fears, triggering a risk-off rotation, and potentially forcing the Fed to consider rate hikes. This is the single largest exogenous shock risk.

2. **🟠 Federal Reserve Policy Surprise (MEDIUM-HIGH):** With PCE at 3.5% and energy prices elevated, the probability of a rate hike by year-end has risen to ~30%. Any Fed communication that shifts the market's terminal rate expectations upward would cause a broad multiple compression — particularly painful for high-P/E AI and software names.

3. **🟠 Broadcom Earnings Miss (MEDIUM):** Tonight's AVGO report is the week's pivotal data point. AI semiconductor bulls have priced in $10.7B in AI revenue. A miss — or more importantly, weak forward guidance — could trigger a broad semiconductor/AI selloff given how crowded the positioning is.

4. **🟡 Taiwan Geopolitical Escalation (MEDIUM — long-dated):** Any deterioration in cross-strait relations affecting TSMC operations would be catastrophic for the entire global semiconductor supply chain. This is a low-probability, extremely high-impact tail risk that should inform position sizing in TSM and Taiwan-dependent chipmakers.

5. **🟡 AI Capex Cycle Deceleration (MEDIUM — developing):** The hyperscaler $725 billion capex commitment has been the primary bull catalyst. Any earnings report or management commentary suggesting a reduction in AI infrastructure spending would ripple through the entire supply chain — Broadcom, Marvell, TSMC, Applied Materials, and Micron would all reprice lower.

## Bottom Line

The AI infrastructure supercycle remains the dominant market force, with record S&P 500 levels and a historic software rally confirming that the bull case is intact — but investors face a compression of margin for error as valuations stretch and macro headwinds (sticky inflation, geopolitical risk premium, rate-hike optionality) accumulate simultaneously. The fundamental framework remains constructive: buy the picks-and-shovels layer of AI (TSMC, Broadcom, Applied Materials, Micron) where valuations are anchored by real earnings growth, and remain selective in high-multiple software names where AI monetization must still be proven. **Tonight's Broadcom earnings are the single most important near-term catalyst** — a beat-and-raise would validate the AI infrastructure narrative and likely pull the entire sector higher; a miss would test conviction across the board.
