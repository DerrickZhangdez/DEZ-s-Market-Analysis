# Weekly Market Preview — Week of June 8 to June 12, 2026

**Published:** Sunday, June 7, 2026 (overnight futures open) | Prepared by DEZ Market Analysis

---

## The Week in One Paragraph

Markets enter the week of June 8 on the defensive after a violent Friday reversal that knocked the S&P 500 2.6% lower and the Nasdaq 4.2% — the worst single-day loss since the winter sell-off — as a blowout May jobs report (172,000 vs. ~86,000 consensus) crushed rate-cut hopes and sent the 10-year Treasury yield surging to 4.54%. The week-prior euphoria of a fresh S&P all-time high near 7,600, propelled by a software earnings beat cycle, gave way to a dual body blow: Alphabet's $85 billion equity raise to fund AI infrastructure jolted sentiment, and Broadcom's decision to leave its full-year AI chip guidance unchanged despite doubling AI revenue sent AVGO shares down ~20% and dragged the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index lower. The dominant themes for this week are simple and sequential: **CPI on Wednesday morning** will either validate the "inflation re-acceleration" narrative birthed by the jobs report or give markets a relief rally; **the ECB rate decision on Thursday** (markets price a 99% probability of a 25 bps hike to 2.25%) will tighten the global policy screws further; and **Oracle (Wednesday) and Adobe (Thursday) earnings** will serve as the read-through on enterprise AI monetization after Broadcom's mixed message. With new Fed Chair Kevin Warsh — sworn in May 22, hawkish by reputation — preparing for his first FOMC meeting next week (June 16–17), the stakes of this week's CPI print could not be higher. The VIX closed Friday at 21.50, up nearly 40% in a single session, flagging that complacency has been violently disrupted.

## This Week's Critical Calendar

### Federal Reserve Watch

**FOMC Meeting Status: NO meeting this week.**
The next FOMC meeting is **June 16–17, 2026** — an SEP (Summary of Economic Projections) meeting, which includes the updated dot plot and fresh economic projections. This is the highest-impact Fed meeting of any given quarter because markets will get the clearest signal on the rate path under Warsh's new leadership. The June 16–17 meeting is also Warsh's **first as Fed Chair**, having been sworn in May 22 following his Senate confirmation (54–45, the most divisive in Fed history).

**Current Fed Funds Rate:** 3.50%–3.75%

**CME FedWatch Probabilities (as of June 5, 2026):**

| Meeting | Hold | Hike +25bps | Cut -25bps |
|---|---|---|---|
| June 16–17 | ~86% | ~11% | ~3% |
| July | ~80% | ~18% | ~2% |
| December | ~30% | ~40–70% | ~<5% |

**What to listen for this week:** Although there is no scheduled Warsh press conference, **Fed speakers may emerge** in the days before the June 16–17 meeting blackout period begins. Markets will parse any commentary from FOMC members on whether Friday's 172K payroll print shifts the balance toward a July or September hike. The debate inside the Fed — what CNBC described as a "big family fight" — is between doves who want to see more inflation data and hawks (likely aligned with Warsh) who believe the energy-driven CPI surge justifies a hike. A rate hike by year-end is now priced at 40–70% probability. *This week's CPI number will be the single most important input into that debate.*

## Sector Themes to Watch

### AI & Technology

The defining event of the prior week was **Alphabet's $85 billion equity raise** for AI infrastructure — the largest equity offering in U.S. history. This signals an extraordinary commitment to AI capex but simultaneously raised concerns about dilution and whether hyperscaler AI monetization can keep pace with investment. Combined with **Broadcom's flat guidance** despite $10.8B in AI chip revenue (a doubling YoY), the market is now in "show me the money" mode on AI ROI. The narrative has shifted: AI infrastructure spending is real and accelerating, but near-term guidance visibility remains limited. **Oracle's Wednesday report** will be the first direct test of enterprise AI demand post-Broadcom disappointment.

### Hardware & Semiconductors

The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index fell sharply Friday on the Broadcom-Nvidia-CrowdStrike triple-header of negative catalysts. Key dynamics to monitor this week:

- **NVIDIA (NVDA):** After a 6% drop Friday and multiple analyst upgrades citing expanded Blackwell H200/B200 shipments in H2 2026, NVDA enters this week at a reset entry point. The stock has no earnings this week but will trade on the Oracle/CPI macro.
- **Intel (INTC):** Up 250% YTD on a remarkable turnaround and upgrade cycle; analysts are rotating into INTC from NVDA as the "value AI chip play." Watch for any manufacturing update.
- **Broadcom (AVGO):** Down ~20% from its post-earnings level. The stock may see technical support buying given the hyperscaler design wins (Anthropic, Google, Meta, OpenAI) that set up strong revenue in 2026–2027. The question is whether management's guidance conservatism continues.
- **Memory (Micron/LRCX):** Micron earnings June 24 (not this week); memory pricing cycle remains the backdrop.

### Software & SaaS

Adobe (ADBE) reports Thursday. The software complex had been the strongest earnings season driver through May, with beat rates above 80%. The debate entering June: Is generative AI a tailwind (enhanced product, AI-driven pricing power) or headwind (AI-native competitors commoditizing design) for Adobe? The company's $25B buyback and 10%+ ARR growth guidance suggest management confidence. A miss here, particularly on guidance, would be disproportionately negative for the broader SaaS complex given its bellwether status.

### Energy & Commodities

Oil prices remain volatile and elevated in the $90–97 range for Brent. OPEC+ announced a 188,000 bpd output increase for June (slightly less than May's 206K). The UAE officially departed OPEC effective May 1, 2026, complicating the cartel's cohesion. Chinese crude imports fell to a 10-year low, creating downward pressure. The key wildcard: **any diplomatic progress on the Strait of Hormuz** could release significant selling pressure across energy, lowering CPI expectations and giving the Fed room to hold — a highly positive scenario for equities.

Natural gas prices will track European energy demand ahead of summer, with ECB's Thursday decision implicitly acknowledging that energy-driven inflation is structural near-term.

### Financials & Rates

The yield curve is now upward-sloping (steepening) for the first time in years:
- 2Y: 4.17% | 10Y: 4.54% | 30Y: 5.01%

This is a meaningful regime change. Steepening curves benefit bank net interest margins (positive for C, JPM, WFC, BAC) but compress growth stock multiples. The 10Y at 4.54% is now near the level that triggered the 2023 multiple compression. If CPI Wednesday comes in hot, 10Y could test 4.75%+, which would be a genuine threat to equity multiples, particularly in tech. The 30Y Treasury at 5.01% is a significant barrier for housing, putting Lennar's Thursday earnings in focus.

## Positioning & Strategy

**Pre-CPI positioning (Monday–Tuesday):** Markets will be in a holding pattern through Tuesday as participants position for Wednesday's CPI. Expect light volume with a bearish bias in the overnight to Tuesday session, as the risk-reward of holding large tech long positions into a potentially hot CPI is unfavorable. Consider trimming high-multiple names (>35x forward P/E) ahead of Wednesday morning. Cash is a position. If you missed the Friday selloff, the setup for tactical shorts on rate-sensitive names (housing, utilities, long-duration tech) is elevated heading into the inflation print.

**CPI reaction playbook:** A core CPI reading at or below +0.2% MoM (the consensus) is likely to produce a sharp relief rally — the "inflation is peaking" narrative reasserts, Warsh signals patience at the June 16–17 meeting, and the S&P could recover 1.5–2.5% intraday. In this scenario, selectively buy dips in Oracle, NVDA, and AVGO. A core CPI reading of +0.3% or higher triggers the opposite: 10Y tests 4.75%, Nasdaq under pressure, and the question shifts to whether Warsh hikes at June 16–17 (currently ~11% probability, which would spike to perhaps 30–40%). In the hot-CPI scenario, energy and financials (banks benefit from steeper curves) are the defensive repositioning plays.

**Earnings rotation (Wednesday–Thursday):** Oracle and Adobe have the potential to rewrite the AI earnings narrative after Broadcom's guidance disappointment. If both companies deliver beat-and-raise results with strong AI contract RPO and ARR growth respectively, the tech sector could recover independently of the macro backdrop. This has happened before — software companies insulating the index from semiconductor sentiment swings. Oracle in particular is structurally underowned relative to its actual AI cloud infrastructure role; a breakout earnings report could be a multi-session catalyst.

**Sector positioning for the week:** Overweight **Energy** (oil demand normalization as Strait of Hormuz re-opens), **Financials** (steepening yield curve benefits bank NIMs), and **Select AI infrastructure** (NVDA, ORCL on dips). Underweight **High-duration software** (until CPI clears), **Homebuilders/REITs** (rate sensitivity), and **Consumer discretionary** (stagflationary pressure). **Intel (INTC)** is the most interesting "value AI" rotation into a market environment that is punishing stretched multiples.

## Bottom Line

**The single most important call this week is simple: Wednesday's May CPI print is binary for the month of June.** A core CPI at or below consensus (+0.2% MoM) buys Warsh time to hold steady at the June 16–17 FOMC, relieves bond market pressure, and likely reverses much of Friday's selloff — making Oracle, Adobe, NVDA, and AVGO tactical buys ahead of and into that release. A core CPI above +0.3% accelerates rate hike pricing, pressures growth multiples further, and makes the June 16–17 FOMC the riskiest Fed meeting since 2022. Position accordingly: size down large growth exposures into Tuesday's close, wait for the CPI print, and react rather than predict. The best trades this week are made on Wednesday between 8:30 a.m. and 11:00 a.m. ET.
