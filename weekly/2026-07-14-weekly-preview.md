**Published:** Sunday, July 13, 2026 | Overnight futures open

## The Week in One Paragraph

The week of July 14 kicks off what may be the most consequential stretch of the Q2 2026 earnings season. Five major U.S. banks report simultaneously on Tuesday morning — JPMorgan, Citigroup, Wells Fargo, BlackRock, and more — immediately followed by the June CPI print at 8:30 ET. As if that weren't enough, the freshly confirmed Fed Chair Kevin Warsh delivers his debut semiannual congressional testimony to the House (Tuesday) and Senate (Wednesday) just as inflation data is digested by markets. TSMC reports Thursday in what will be the defining AI-infrastructure readthrough of the quarter, while ASML, Goldman Sachs, Bank of America, and UnitedHealth round out an earnings slate expected to confirm a second straight quarter of 20%+ S&P 500 profit growth. The macro backdrop is constructive — inflation is decelerating, geopolitical tail risks have eased since the June 18 US-Iran MOU, and futures are drifting modestly higher Sunday evening — but valuations remain stretched and any hawkish signal from Warsh or a hotter-than-expected CPI could quickly shift sentiment.

## Sector Themes to Watch

### AI & Technology

The AI trade is back in full force and **this week it must deliver**. TSMC's July 16 report is the single most important data point for the entire AI complex in Q2. The company has guided for strong demand and gross margin expansion driven by the transition from generative AI to agentic AI — which is requiring substantially more compute per inference. Big Tech stocks have outperformed in July while memory stocks pulled back from highs. The key narrative question: is AI capex demand front-loaded (bad for 2H) or structural (good for everything)? TSMC's tone on CoWoS capacity and N3/N2 bookings will answer that.

### Hardware & Semiconductors

ASML reporting Wednesday is critical for the EUV litho equipment cycle. Semiconductor stocks broadly have ripped in July amid renewed optimism, but the SOX index still needs to prove itself technically. TSMC's forward guidance on leading-edge wafer starts will determine whether the SOX breaks out or stalls. Watch for commentary on TSMC's Arizona fab ramp and whether advanced packaging (CoWoS, SoIC) can keep up with Nvidia's B-series demand. Memory stocks have pulled back after June's highs — look for stabilizing signals in any supply/demand commentary.

### Software & SaaS

Software faces an existential narrative challenge this earnings season: can it prove its relevance in the age of AI agents? Markets are watching for whether enterprise software companies can demonstrate AI-driven pricing power or whether they risk commoditization. Guidance is the key metric — any softening on ARR growth or net retention rates will be punished. AI disruption fears are present and software will need to put up solid beats with strong guidance to quiet them.

### Energy & Commodities

WTI at ~$72/bbl is structurally pressured by recovering Gulf supply, OPEC+ production normalization, and soft global demand. Nat gas is a separate story — data center power demand is creating structural tailwinds for domestic supply. Metals related to tech supply chains (copper, cobalt, rare earths) remain in focus as US-China critical mineral tensions persist. Energy sector earnings may disappoint given lower realized prices vs. Q2 2025.

### Financials & Rates

This is **the week** for financials. Five major banks report simultaneously Tuesday, creating the largest single-day bank earnings event in memory. The trifecta of higher investment banking activity, strong trading revenues (particularly fixed income and equities), and recovering fee income sets up for broadly constructive results. However, net interest income outlooks and loan growth guidance will determine whether stocks can hold post-earnings gains. With the 10-year at 4.56% and the yield curve normalizing, banks' NII trajectories are improving — but markets have already priced much of this. Goldilocks scenario: beats + raised full-year guidance + stable credit quality = financials leading the broad market higher.

## Valuation-Based Stock Ideas for the Week

### 1. Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing (TSM)
- **Catalyst this week:** Q2 earnings July 16; guidance for Q3 and full-year AI demand
- **Valuation:** Forward P/E ~24x, EV/EBITDA ~18x, P/S ~10x
- **Why attractive:** TSMC has beaten EPS estimates in each of the past four quarters by an average of 8.34%. Revenue expected to jump from $30B to $40B YoY (+33%). With AI chip demand "extremely robust" and no credible alternative foundry for N3/N2 volumes, TSMC's pricing power and margin expansion story is intact. At current levels, investors get the de facto AI infrastructure monopoly at a discount to US-listed AI plays.
- **Key risk:** Any softer-than-guided Q3 revenue outlook or capacity constraint signals; geopolitical Taiwan risk premium
- **Rating: Strong Buy**

### 2. Goldman Sachs (GS)
- **Catalyst this week:** Q2 earnings July 15; expected EPS ~$14.47, +32% YoY
- **Valuation:** Forward P/E ~12x, P/B ~1.6x
- **Why attractive:** Goldman is poised for a blockbuster quarter driven by trading revenues and M&A advisory fees. The options market is pricing in a ±6% move — but the asymmetry favors upside given the trading environment. Goldman has been quietly rebuilding its M&A pipeline and asset management revenues while shedding the Marcus consumer banking drag. At 12x forward earnings, it's cheap relative to the quality of its franchise.
- **Key risk:** Litigation charges; management commentary on capital return; weaker-than-expected advisory pipeline
- **Rating: Buy**

### 3. Alphabet (GOOGL)
- **Catalyst this week:** No earnings this week (reports later in July), but TSMC results and AI capex narrative will re-rate AI beneficiaries including Google Cloud
- **Valuation:** Forward P/E ~21x, EV/EBITDA ~15x, P/S ~6x — 44 Buy / 14 Strong Buy ratings, zero Sells
- **Why attractive:** Analyst price targets cluster $414–$442, implying 16–24% upside from current levels. Google Cloud growth is re-accelerating and Gemini integration across Search, Workspace, and Cloud is just beginning to monetize. Alphabet is one of the few mega-caps with valuation support AND AI earnings optionality. The TSMC readthrough should be a catalyst for Alphabet pre-earnings.
- **Key risk:** DOJ antitrust remedies; AI Search cannibalization
- **Rating: Buy**

### 4. Cigna Group (CI)
- **Catalyst this week:** Sector catalyst from UNH earnings (July 16) — a clean UNH print lifts all managed care
- **Valuation:** Forward P/E ~11x, EV/EBITDA ~9x — Bernstein PT $381, ~30.6% upside
- **Why attractive:** Cigna trades at a steep discount to its managed care peers and broader market. The stock has been weighed down by sector-wide Medical Loss Ratio anxiety and DOJ scrutiny (primarily on UNH). A clean UNH print July 16 would relieve the overhang and redirect attention to Cigna's superior Evernorth pharmacy/benefits business. At 11x earnings, the margin of safety is substantial.
- **Key risk:** UNH earnings disappoint; Medicare Advantage regulatory headwinds spread
- **Rating: Accumulate**

### 5. ASML Holding (ASML)
- **Catalyst this week:** Q2 earnings July 15; EUV order backlog update
- **Valuation:** Forward P/E ~30x, EV/EBITDA ~22x — premium justified by monopoly position
- **Why attractive:** ASML is the only company in the world capable of producing EUV lithography machines, which are essential for sub-5nm chip production. Every leading-edge fab expansion — TSMC Arizona, Intel 18A, Samsung — requires ASML machines. With a 12-18 month lead time and a backlog stretching into 2027-2028, ASML's revenue visibility is exceptional. The stock has lagged TSMC this year and offers a catch-up trade into earnings.
- **Key risk:** Order cancellations or delays from Samsung/Intel fab struggles; China export restrictions compressing total addressable market
- **Rating: Buy**

## Positioning & Strategy

**The bull case is alive but needs confirmation.** S&P 500 earnings are tracking for 23.3% growth in Q2 — the second consecutive quarter above 20%. If big banks clear the bar on Tuesday and TSMC echoes on Thursday, the fundamental narrative remains intact. The seasonal tailwind in July is real and historically bullish. Lean into it selectively.

**CPI is the fulcrum.** Tuesday at 8:30 ET will either validate the soft-landing thesis or resurrect stagflation fears. The consensus expects headline CPI to ease to 3.9% YoY driven by lower energy prices. Core at 2.9% is still sticky and above the Fed's 2% target. Markets are pricing a constructive outcome. If core CPI surprises above 3.0%, the Treasury yield backup could be swift — 10-year toward 4.70% is not inconceivable, and that would compress tech multiples meaningfully into options expiration.

**Financials are the rotation trade.** If banks beat and raise guidance, the rotation from expensive AI/semiconductor names into undervalued financials has legs. Goldman Sachs at 12x forward earnings is objectively cheap for a franchise of its quality. JPMorgan's capital return capacity is underappreciated. Overweight financials relative to the index as a hedge against a rates-driven tech selloff: if yields rise, tech falls but bank NII benefits; if yields fall, tech wins but banks still report great Q2s.

---

## Bottom Line

**TSMC on Thursday is the alpha event of the quarter.** If Taiwan Semi confirms that AI chip demand is "extremely robust" — and guides Q3 revenue to $42B+ — the bull market in AI hardware has legs through year-end and the S&P 500 breaks above 7,600. If TSMC disappoints or guides cautiously, brace for a broader de-risking across the AI complex regardless of what banks reported earlier in the week. Position into Tuesday's CPI with reduced gross exposure, add back selectively on a clean print, and hold TSMC into Thursday for the definitive read on the AI infrastructure cycle.
