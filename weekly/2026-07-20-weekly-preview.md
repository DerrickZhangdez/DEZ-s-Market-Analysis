# Weekly Market Preview — Week of July 21 to July 25, 2026

**Published:** Sunday, July 20, 2026 | Overnight futures open

---

## The Week in One Paragraph

Wall Street enters the week of July 21 licking wounds from a bruising prior week — the S&P 500 shed 1.6%, the Nasdaq cratered 2.9%, and the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index fell 11%, driven 24% below its late-June all-time highs as investors questioned the ROI timeline of AI infrastructure spending. Yet this week's calendar offers a decisive referendum: Alphabet and Tesla report Wednesday evening, Intel reports Thursday, and the ECB delivers its policy decision Thursday afternoon. These events collectively will answer the question the market has been asking all month — can mega-cap earnings validate the AI capital expenditure cycle, or does the chip-stock correction deepen into a broader tech rout? Geopolitical tension around the Strait of Hormuz continues to inject an energy premium into oil prices, while the 10-year Treasury at 4.55% and a Fed holding at 3.5–3.75% (next FOMC: July 28–29) keep rate risk alive heading into July's final stretch.

## Sector Themes to Watch

### AI & Technology
The central question of the week: **does Alphabet's CapEx guidance justify the AI buildout trade, or does it signal diminishing urgency?** Analysts expect Alphabet to announce another significant CapEx increase (quarter-over-quarter and year-over-year), with Google Cloud continuing to accelerate above 30% growth. Any pullback in CapEx language would be read as demand signal weakness for GPU/HBM/networking suppliers. Alphabet's AI search product (AI Overviews) monetization progress is an additional key watch — analysts want evidence that AI-enhanced search is expanding revenue per query rather than cannibalizing traditional paid search.

**AMD's "Advancing AI" event also looms next week** — any pre-event channel checks or commentary from Texas Instruments and ServiceNow on AI workload trends will set early expectations.

### Hardware & Semiconductors
The Philly Semiconductor Index has entered technical bear market territory (down 24% from late-June highs). Intel reports Thursday after a dramatic 21% decline over the past seven trading sessions — partly driven by SK Hynix's reported decision to slow HBM production expansion. **Key questions for Intel:** (1) Is foundry customer pipeline accelerating? (2) Are AI PC refresh cycles translating to unit volume? (3) What is management's view on HBM demand from hyperscalers? Semiconductor sales grew 79% YoY in Q1 2026; BNP Paribas expects 132% YoY growth in Q2 2026, but the pace of estimate revisions will matter more than the number itself at these valuations.

**Texas Instruments is the sleeper of the week** — as an analog semiconductor bellwether, its inventory channel commentary and end-market demand color will give investors the first read on whether the industrial/auto semiconductor recovery is on track heading into H2 2026.

### Software & SaaS
ServiceNow reports Wednesday and will be scrutinized for two data points: (1) enterprise AI workflow seat expansion (its "Now Assist" AI product), and (2) renewal rates and deal sizes in a more cautious IT spending environment. IBM's watsonx momentum and hybrid cloud growth (Wednesday) provides a second checkpoint. **The software sector has largely been insulated from the hardware selloff**, but if enterprise AI adoption proves slower to monetize than expected, software multiples could compress from currently elevated levels.

### Energy & Commodities
Brent crude trading near $82.50 after a 4.5% single-day spike on July 17 amid Hormuz tensions. OPEC+ has announced a fifth consecutive monthly output increase (188,000 bpd from August), providing a structural ceiling for prices. The IEA notes global oil supply rebounded 4.1 mb/d in June as Hormuz flows partially resumed, but product cracks and refining margins remain elevated. **Energy and commodity inflation is the key macro risk to watch** — if Hormuz disruptions worsen, inflation expectations will ratchet higher, the Fed will feel pressure to hike, and equity multiples will compress sharply. Halliburton's earnings Tuesday will give visibility on oil services demand and upstream capital discipline.

**For tech investors:** Copper, rare earths, and memory chip pricing are embedded in AI infrastructure costs. Any OPEC or geopolitical shock that drives energy costs higher compresses data center economics for hyperscalers.

### Financials & Rates
The 10-year Treasury yield ended last week at 4.55%, down from a two-month high of 4.62% on July 13. The DXY dollar index is at ~100.91, off early-July highs near 101.39. Capital One reports Tuesday and will be the week's primary read on U.S. consumer credit quality — delinquency trends, net charge-off rates, and any guidance commentary on credit provisioning. Credit spreads remain the key canary: if corporate spreads widen materially this week, it signals that the equity multiple compression in tech could spread to broader market risk-off. **The yield curve posture (with the Fed on hold at 3.5–3.75% and rate hike odds building for year-end) is compressing equity PE multiples, particularly for high-duration growth names.**

## Positioning & Strategy

**Earnings are the only game in town this week.** With no major U.S. macro releases, the Fed in blackout, and the ECB widely expected to hold, every basis point of price action in mega-cap tech will be driven by Wednesday and Thursday earnings. Position sizing into Wednesday's close should be treated as binary event risk — both Alphabet and Tesla have historically moved 5–8% after earnings in either direction, and this quarter the stakes are amplified by the broader AI narrative at a critical juncture.

**For aggressive positioning:** A long Alphabet (GOOGL) into earnings paired with a short Philadelphia Semiconductor Index ETF (SOXX) hedges the AI theme against the hardware selloff — capturing the upside if Alphabet confirms CapEx (which benefits software/platform names) while hedging against any further chip sector weakness if CapEx guidance disappoints on the margin. Texas Instruments is the week's highest-conviction non-event trade: buying into a historically cheap analog cycle bottom with a catalyst-rich Q2 report.

**For defensive positioning:** With VIX at 18.77 and the market sitting on key technical support, buying protective puts into earnings week is expensive but rational. Alternatively, rotating within tech from hardware/semiconductor names (SOXX, NVDA) to software/platform names (GOOGL, NOW, CRM) positions for a scenario where AI's center of gravity continues shifting from "chips" to "applications." Energy and defense remain geopolitical hedges — HAL and NOC offer fundamental support even if equity markets sell off further.

**Watch the credit markets Thursday and Friday.** If the ECB holds and Alphabet/Tesla beat, but corporate credit spreads widen rather than tighten, the equity market will have a problem — it would suggest institutional investors are de-risking at the portfolio level despite the positive earnings news. In that scenario, take profits on any Wednesday/Thursday bounce.

## Bottom Line

**The week of July 21 is an earnings verdict on the AI trade.** Alphabet must confirm robust Cloud growth and sustained CapEx commitment on Wednesday evening — if it does, expect a sharp reversal of last week's semiconductor selloff and a test of S&P 7,600. If CapEx guidance disappoints or Cloud growth decelerates, the Philly Semi bear market will deepen, and the S&P risks testing 7,000–7,050 by month-end. Position for the upside scenario (Alphabet has 44 Buy ratings, zero Sells), but hedge with SOXX puts and a tight stop on any longs if Wednesday night's print misses on the CapEx dimension. The ECB hold Thursday is noise; the Hormuz situation is the dominant geopolitical risk to watch daily. **The single most important call: buy Alphabet (GOOGL) into earnings with disciplined risk management — this is the week the AI trade either restarts or breaks down.**
