## The Week in One Paragraph

Markets return from the July 4th holiday weekend digesting a deeply disappointing June jobs report — just 57,000 nonfarm payrolls versus a 115,000 consensus — that confirmed the labor market is cooling faster than expected. The S&P 500 sits near 7,500 after a tech-led selloff that has dragged the Magnificent 7 down more than 12% month-to-date, even as the semiconductor sector still sits up ~47% year-to-date on AI infrastructure euphoria. This is a **light calendar week**, dominated by Wednesday's FOMC minutes from Chair Kevin Warsh's first June meeting, which markets will parse for clues on the September rate-cut path now pricing at 60–65% probability. Geopolitically, the US–Iran ceasefire signed June 17 and Strait of Hormuz reopening have defused the energy crisis that briefly pushed Brent above $100 — crude is back near $69 — but investors must still contend with fragile US–China tech-sector tensions and Russia–Ukraine diplomatic uncertainty. The setup favors a cautious relief rally early in the week, subject to any surprise hawkish signal from Wednesday's Fed minutes.

## Sector Themes to Watch

### AI & Technology
The AI infrastructure trade remains structurally intact even as the Magnificent 7 pullback (-12.7% MTD) creates near-term turbulence. Broadcom's 143% YoY AI revenue growth in Q2 is the most recent proof point. Investor focus this week: **Apple–Intel deal rumors** (chipset partnership) and any developments around **OpenAI's delayed IPO**. No major AI product launches are confirmed, but capex guidance language from upcoming earnings (Microsoft on July 29) is being pre-positioned. Watch for any analyst notes updating AI capex curves — this will set tone for the AI basket.

### Hardware & Semiconductors
The PHLX Semiconductor Index surged ~88% in Q2 2026 — the strongest quarterly gain since 1994 — but has given back ~7% in recent sessions as profit-taking accelerated. The AI buildout narrative remains the primary demand driver. Key focus: US–China export controls on advanced chips (any new Commerce Dept. announcements), Taiwan Semiconductor's pricing signals, and memory pricing trends (HBM for AI applications remains tight). The Apple–Intel deal rumor, if confirmed, would be a significant positive catalyst for domestic chip supply chains.

### Software & SaaS
No major software earnings this week. The sector is navigating a bifurcated narrative: companies embedding AI features are commanding premium multiples, while traditional SaaS without a credible AI story faces multiple compression. Analyst calls and price target changes from the sell-side will drive stock-specific moves. Watch for any conference presentations or investor day announcements.

### Energy & Commodities
Oil is at ~$69/barrel (WTI), down sharply from the $100+ spike during the Hormuz crisis. OPEC+ continues adding supply — its **fourth consecutive quota hike**, adding ~188K bpd for July (cumulative ~600K bpd since April). Saudi Arabia crude exports are back to ~90% of pre-war levels; UAE fully restored. Q4 analyst forecasts for WTI range from $70–$80. **This week: watch for any OPEC+ rhetoric ahead of their August meeting.** Natural gas and LNG markets are also stabilizing. For tech supply chains, rare earth/critical minerals remain a US–China friction point.

### Financials & Rates
The 10-year Treasury sits at **4.49%** with the 2-year at **4.14%** — the yield curve has partially re-inverted, reflecting the "higher-for-longer" overhang against the weak jobs data. Bank earnings begin in earnest the week of July 14 (JPMorgan, Citigroup, Wells Fargo). This week, credit spreads and bank loan demand data will attract attention. A hawkish surprise from Wednesday's FOMC minutes would push the 10-year toward 4.60–4.70%, potentially capping equity P/E multiples at current levels. A dovish surprise could push the 10-year toward 4.30%, providing meaningful multiple expansion support for high-duration growth names.

## Positioning & Strategy

**The Dominant Bet This Week Is Wednesday's Minutes.** The weak jobs report (+57K) has the market leaning dovish and pricing in a September cut at 60–65% probability. If the June FOMC minutes reveal a Fed (under Warsh) that was more hawkish than the hold decision implied — concerned about tariff-driven inflation and reluctant to commit to a cut path — the 2-year yield gaps higher and tech/growth stocks face renewed selling pressure. Conversely, if the minutes reveal a committee genuinely alarmed by labor market deterioration, the September cut probability jumps to 75–80%, the 10-year tests 4.30%, and high-duration growth assets rally hard.

**Preferred Positioning:** Given asymmetric risk around the minutes, consider a dumbbell approach: overweight defensive dividend growers (PEP, ORLY, ACGL) that benefit from a rate-cut path while providing downside cushion if growth concerns dominate; pair with a modest position in rate-sensitive tech (semiconductors on the pullback, particularly NVDA/AVGO which have dipped from all-time highs) for the event where dovish minutes ignite the July rally.

**Hedges Worth Considering:** VIX at 15.81 makes protection cheap. July 22nd VIX calls at the 20 strike offer inexpensive tail-risk coverage into the July 28–29 FOMC meeting. S&P 500 put spreads at 7,300/7,100 for July expiration offer defined-risk downside protection at a reasonable cost.

**Sector Rotation Watch:** The rotation OUT of mega-cap tech (Magnificent 7 -12.7% MTD) and INTO defensives and value is underway. July seasonality historically favors the first week of the month before earnings season catalysts drive re-concentration. If Q2 earnings confirm AI capex discipline (starting with Microsoft/Amazon in late July), the tech recovery trade has legs; if earnings disappoint on AI monetization, the rotation to value deepens.

---

## Bottom Line

**The single most important call for this week: Wednesday's FOMC minutes at 2 PM ET are a binary event.** A dovish read accelerates the September cut pricing and re-ignites the AI-growth trade; a hawkish surprise (Warsh signaling inflation vigilance trumps jobs weakness) extends the tech selloff and strengthens the defensive rotation. Position ahead with hedges, and consider adding to high-quality value names (TW, ORLY, PEP) on any Monday/Tuesday dip before the minutes drop.
