# Weekly Market Preview — Week of June 22, 2026 to June 26, 2026

**Published:** Sunday, June 21, 2026 | Overnight futures open

---

## The Week in One Paragraph

U.S. equity markets enter the week in recovery mode following the June 16–17 FOMC meeting, where new Fed Chair Kevin Warsh delivered a hawkish surprise — holding rates at 3.50%–3.75% and signaling **no cuts for all of 2026** — sending stocks lower on Friday. Sunday night futures are pointing modestly lower, with S&P contracts off ~0.4% as investors weigh two countervailing forces: the fragile U.S.–Iran ceasefire framework (which briefly cooled oil prices but is already showing cracks after President Trump renewed threats against Tehran on Sunday) and Thursday's May PCE print, the week's macro centerpiece. Micron Technology (MU) reports Wednesday after the close — the marquee earnings event — with Wall Street expecting a near-tenfold EPS surge that will serve as a critical read on the AI memory supercycle. Against this backdrop of elevated rates, a hawkish Fed, headline geopolitical risk, and powerful AI capex momentum, the week demands defensive agility paired with selective growth exposure around high-conviction catalysts.

| Issue | Status | Market Impact |

| **Russia-Ukraine** | Ukraine maintaining sovereignty; NATO rearmament accelerating; no near-term major escalation | **MEDIUM** — European energy, defense sector |

**The Iran file is the dominant geopolitical wildcard this week.** The June 15 MoU established a 60-day ceasefire framework and Strait of Hormuz reopening accord — oil fell ~8% last week toward ~$77/bbl in response, and Hormuz shipments have recovered from a May low of ~9.6 mb/d to ~12 mb/d. However, Trump's Sunday warning to Iran and Netanyahu's public dispute of the Lebanon ceasefire terms inject fragility into the MoU's durability. Key things to watch: (1) Any formal Trump administration statement Monday on the state of the accord; (2) Progress on the 60-day window for nuclear talks, including Iran's uranium dilution commitments; (3) Lebanese front — the greatest risk of derailment per experts. A breakdown in the MoU would send oil sharply higher, re-energize inflation fears, and trigger broad equity risk-off that would compound Thursday's PCE exposure.

**US-China:** The U.S. continues to seek comprehensive semiconductor and AI supply chain decoupling from China. Trump's tariffs and Chinese manufacturing overcapacity pose dual threats to the global production network. Export control enforcement on advanced AI chips (Nvidia H-series, ASML EUV equipment) tightened further in Q2. Watch for any executive orders or Commerce Department actions this week.

## Sector Themes to Watch

### AI & Technology

The AI infrastructure investment thesis remains intact but is entering a phase of execution scrutiny. Micron's HBM (High Bandwidth Memory) results on Wednesday will be parsed not just for the quarter but for forward guidance on AI memory demand. NVIDIA has received multiple price target lifts following expanded Blackwell shipment guidance for H2 2026. The "picks and shovels" trade — memory, power management, advanced packaging, cooling infrastructure — continues to outperform pure-play software AI plays. Broadcom's recent Q2 results showed weak traditional software sales alongside stable AI chip forecasts, a microcosm of the emerging divergence within tech.

### Hardware & Semiconductors

Memory is in a supply-constrained, demand-surging cycle. Micron's projected 81% gross margin reflects this dynamic — a historic high that reflects both pricing power and cost discipline. DRAM tightness and limited likelihood of oversupply through 2027 (Goldman Sachs, Wedbush) provide durable fundamental support. Intel's upgrade wave is the most notable rotation story of 2026: multiple previously bearish analysts have moved to Hold or Buy in June, suggesting the market is beginning to price in an IFS (Intel Foundry Services) recovery scenario supported by U.S. government strategic interest. Reports of an Apple-Intel supply deal have added fuel to this narrative.

### Software & SaaS

Software faces a bifurcated narrative: AI-native platforms and infrastructure orchestration tools are gaining share and pricing power, while legacy SaaS incumbents face growing disruption risk from AI copilots. The sector's valuation multiples remain elevated versus the rate environment, creating vulnerability to any hawkish surprise. Enterprise AI transformation budgets — Accenture's consulting pipeline is a bellwether — remain robust, but execution on ROI is becoming the gating question as firms move from pilot to production deployments.

### Energy & Commodities

Oil at ~$77/bbl is a double-edged sword: low enough to ease inflation pressure compared to Q1 highs, high enough to remain above breakeven for most producers and to sustain a geopolitical premium. IEA's June Oil Market Report flagged a 1.1 mb/d YoY global demand decline for 2026, including a 5 mb/d Q2 plunge driven by high prices and supply disruptions. OPEC+ has been making modest adjustments. The Iran MoU's stability is the key variable for oil this week. Natural gas prices in Europe remain elevated given Russia supply constraints. For tech supply chains, watch rare earth and advanced chip material import headlines given US-China friction.

### Financials & Rates

The 10-year Treasury at ~4.46–4.49% is at the high end of its recent range, with the 2-year at 4.20% (10Y-2Y spread: +27 bps). The yield curve is mildly positive — no longer inverted, historically signaling ebbed recession risk — but the gap is narrow and fragile. The DXY dollar index at ~100.85 (near its 52-week high of 101.13) reflects ongoing hawkish Fed pricing and safe-haven demand from geopolitical uncertainty. For equity valuations, the near-parity between the S&P 500's forward earnings yield and the 10Y Treasury remains a key structural vulnerability: it implies a near-zero equity risk premium, leaving stocks exposed to any upward yield surprise from Thursday's PCE.

## Positioning & Strategy

**Hold the line, but know your triggers.** The FOMC's hawkish surprise last week creates a difficult tactical environment: the market must digest "no cuts in 2026" against a backdrop of still-resilient corporate earnings (S&P 500 full-year EPS growth forecast: +25%). The S&P 500 at ~7,500 remains historically stretched on valuation — forward earnings yield near parity with 10-year Treasuries implies a minimal equity risk premium. The path of least resistance for the week is **sideways-to-slightly-lower** until Thursday's PCE clears the air.

**Thursday's PCE is the binary event of the week.** A cool read (core PCE <0.2% MoM, YoY below 3.2%) could spark a short-covering rally in rate-sensitive sectors — homebuilders, utilities, small caps — and would support a broader market recovery into Q2 close. A hot read (core >0.4% MoM) would validate Warsh's hawkish bias, likely push 10-year yields above 4.55%, and hit tech multiples hard. The tactical playbook: trim exposure into the Wednesday Micron print (capture any pre-earnings enthusiasm), and reload Thursday post-PCE — either buying the relief rally or waiting for the selloff to find support at 7,334.

**Maintain geopolitical optionality.** The Iran MoU is fragile, and Sunday's Trump warning is a reminder that the ceasefire's durability is not guaranteed. A 5–8% portfolio allocation to energy (XLE, CVX) or inverse equity protection (SPXU) provides asymmetric insurance against an Iran flare. Oil at $77 already reflects partial peace-deal discount — a breakdown in the MoU could produce a $10+ oil spike and an immediate equity futures gap down. Don't be fully long into this risk without a hedge in place.

## Bottom Line

**The week's single most important call: Position tactically around Micron (MU) Wednesday and the PCE Thursday — these two events, 14 hours apart, define the week.** Micron is reporting what could be a generational quarter for the memory industry; an 81% gross margin at scale is unprecedented, and AI-driven HBM demand has multi-year runway. Even if results land at the low end of guidance, the thesis remains intact and any selloff is a buying opportunity. Meanwhile, Thursday's PCE is the macro fulcrum: a cool print is the green light for a Q2-end rally and would validate the "soft-landing" narrative; a hot one reopens the question of whether Warsh will hike rather than hold at July's meeting — a scenario the market is not pricing (89% hold probability). Hedge the Iran tail, watch the 7,334 support, and let the data speak.
