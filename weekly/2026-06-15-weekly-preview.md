## The Week in One Paragraph

This week is one of the most consequential in recent memory — and it all unfolds in just four trading days. The dominant event is the **FOMC meeting (June 16–17)**, the first presided over by new Fed Chair **Kevin Warsh**, whose hawkish reputation and inaugural dot plot release will define the market's rate expectations for the rest of 2026. With CPI running at 4.2% year-over-year, hike bets now on the table, and zero probability of a cut priced into any 2026 meeting, the press conference on Wednesday June 17 is the single most important market event of Q2. Layered on top of that is the extraordinary geopolitical situation: **Trump signaled an imminent US-Iran peace deal** on June 11, which has sent Brent crude falling sharply from its May average of $107/bbl toward the high $90s. A formal Hormuz reopening agreement would unleash an estimated 100 million barrels of stranded crude into global markets and send oil prices crashing toward the $70s — with massive ripple effects across energy equities, inflation expectations, and ultimately the Fed's calculus. Meanwhile, **SpaceX (SPCX)** enters only its second week as a publicly traded company following the largest IPO in history ($75 billion), and capital rotation risk is real. Monthly equity OpEx (holiday-adjusted to **Wednesday June 18**) and VIX expiration (**Wednesday June 17**) add compressed volatility to the middle of the week. The setup is asymmetric: a hawkish Warsh surprise could pressure high-multiple tech, but a confirmed Iran peace deal could simultaneously slash inflation expectations and give the Fed room to surprise with a more neutral tone. Buckle up.

**Why This Meeting Is Different:**

Kevin Warsh became Federal Reserve Chair on **May 15, 2026**, replacing Jerome Powell. This is his first FOMC meeting in the chair. Warsh is widely viewed as more hawkish than Powell, having previously criticized the Fed's slow response to inflation. Several things will be closely watched:

1. **The Dot Plot (SEP):** The quarterly Summary of Economic Projections is released at this meeting. With CPI at 4.2% y/y and both consumer and producer inflation at multi-year highs, the dots may show rate hikes penciled in for late 2026 or early 2027 — a significant shift from Powell's easing-bias posture. Even a single dot above 4.00% would shock markets.

2. **Policy Statement Language:** Watch for removal of any residual "easing bias" language. An explicit shift to a "neutral stance" or language acknowledging "upside risk to inflation" would be hawkish and market-moving.

3. **Balance Sheet:** Warsh has a history of favoring aggressive balance sheet reduction. Any acceleration in QT (quantitative tightening) pace announced would pressure long-duration Treasuries and growth equities.

4. **Press Conference Format:** Warsh has not committed to Powell's practice of holding a press conference after every meeting. If he signals he will hold fewer press conferences, that reduces Fed transparency and could itself be a source of market uncertainty.

**Bottom Line:** The rate decision is the sideshow. The dot plot and Warsh's first public communication as Chair are the main event. A hawkish surprise here — even while holding rates steady — could spike the 10Y above 4.75% and reprice high-multiple tech stocks.

### Deep Dive: US-Iran / Strait of Hormuz

This is the single most important geopolitical story for markets heading into this week. Here is the full timeline:

- **February 28, 2026:** Military action began, leading to the de facto closure of the Strait of Hormuz — the world's most critical oil chokepoint.
- **March–May 2026:** Global oil markets entered severe supply shock. Brent crude surged; OECD inventories fell to their lowest level since 2003. Middle East producers cut output by 11 million barrels per day vs. pre-conflict levels.
- **April–May 2026:** Intermittent ceasefire signals and mixed messages from both sides. Brent remained elevated at a May average of $107/bbl.
- **June 11, 2026:** Trump canceled planned strikes against Iran and announced a peace agreement is imminent, calling it an "80% done deal." Brent crude fell more than 5% that day; the S&P 500 surged.
- **June 12 (Friday):** Brent futures for July stood at ~$97.94/bbl — roughly 9% below the prior month's average but still more than a third above pre-war levels. Markets remain cautious.
- **This Week:** The deal has NOT yet been signed. Key terms still being negotiated include the pace of Hormuz reopening, Iran's nuclear program rollback, and economic sanctions relief. If finalized, analysts estimate ~100 million barrels of stranded crude (tankers queued outside Hormuz) will hit the market rapidly, potentially cratering Brent toward $72–78/bbl.

### Software & SaaS

Software faces a more nuanced environment than hardware:
- AI disruption is a genuine two-sided risk: AI tools are increasing developer productivity (positive for growth) but also compressing seat count expansion for legacy software (negative for ARR).
- Accenture (ACN) is flagged as an undervalued play by multiple analysts — AI services implementation demand is structurally high, and ACN's valuation has lagged the sector.
- No major SaaS earnings this week; narrative-driven trading prevails.

### Energy & Commodities

**This sector is in the eye of the storm.** The Iran deal is an existential narrative for energy equities.
- If Hormuz reopens: Major integrated oils (XOM, CVX), E&P names, and energy services stocks face significant downside. The inflation relief from lower oil benefits consumers but hurts energy portfolio weights.
- If deal falls apart: The energy sector becomes the market's defensive safe harbor, with oil potentially surging above $110.
- **OPEC+** already agreed to add 188,000 bpd of output — this is bearish supply-side pressure regardless of Iran.
- **Natural Gas:** If Hormuz reopens and LNG flows normalize from the Middle East, US nat gas prices could face pressure.
- **Watch:** OXY, XOM, CVX, HAL, SLB for post-FOMC/Iran positioning.

### Financials & Rates

- The 10Y Treasury at 4.49% (June 12) is below the psychologically important 4.75% resistance level, but the Warsh FOMC outcome could push it there or beyond.
- **Banks benefit from higher-for-longer** — NIM (net interest margin) expansion continues if hike bets firm up. Regional banks with floating-rate loan books are direct beneficiaries.
- **Credit Spreads:** IG credit spreads remain compressed; HY slightly wider on Iran uncertainty. A confirmed peace deal and oil deflation is actually credit-positive (reduces energy sector default risk).
- **The Equity Risk Premium problem:** With the S&P 500 forward earnings yield now near parity with the 10Y at 4.49%, stocks offer minimal risk premium over risk-free assets. If the 10Y breaks above 4.75–5.00%, the historical pattern suggests a swift 8–12% S&P correction.

## Positioning & Strategy

### The FOMC Trade

The Warsh press conference on Wednesday June 17 is the week's defining moment. The base case (97–99% probability) is a rate hold at 3.50–3.75%. But markets are not going to trade the rate decision itself — they're going to trade every word Warsh says about the path ahead. The dot plot will be the first objective evidence of whether this new Fed is positioned for hikes, holds, or eventual cuts.

**Recommended positioning:** Trim the most rate-sensitive, highest-multiple holdings heading into Wednesday. This means reducing exposure to unprofitable tech, long-duration software, REITs, and utilities before the FOMC announcement. These positions can be re-established after the dust settles Wednesday afternoon if Warsh sounds measured. The trade is asymmetric: a hawkish surprise from Warsh causes 5–8% drawdown in high-multiple names, while a neutral Warsh causes only modest relief rally in those same names (because the base case is already priced in).

**Sector rotation ahead of Wednesday:** Financials (banks benefit from hike signals), short-duration value equities, energy (as a hedge against inflation staying elevated), and cash/T-bills offering 3.5% risk-free.

### The Iran Options

Oil's behavior this week is going to be a direct readout of deal probability. Watch headlines out of diplomatic channels:

- **If Brent breaks below $90 with confirmed Hormuz terms:** Cover energy longs, rotate into consumer discretionary (lower gas prices = more spending) and airlines/transportation.
- **If Brent stays above $95 with no new deal news:** Maintain energy exposure; this is where the market gets choppy because "imminent" deal uncertainty is priced but not resolved.
- **If Trump tweets a deal is signed:** Expect a 3–5% equity rally (S&P tests 7,600) alongside a 10–15% crash in oil. This is the "best of both worlds" scenario for markets — lower inflation, potential Fed pivot back toward neutral, strong consumer.

### The OpEx Compression Setup

With VIX monthly expiration on **Wednesday June 17** and equity monthly OpEx holiday-adjusted to **Wednesday June 18**, the mid-week window will see compressed gamma-driven volatility. Market makers will be hedging aggressively, which typically means amplified moves in either direction. **Wednesday June 17 afternoon** (post-FOMC) through **Wednesday June 18 close** (OpEx) is likely to be the most volatile 24-hour window of the entire year for options positioning. If you're holding weekly options through FOMC, manage size carefully.

**The single most important call for the week:** The Warsh Inauguration at the Fed is the hinge event. If his first dot plot is consistent with Wall Street's expectation of no hikes and eventual easing in 2027, the market shrugs, the Iran deal progress continues to dominate, and equities grind toward new highs.
