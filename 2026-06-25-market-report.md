## Macro & Monetary Environment

**Fed Stance — On Hold, Leaning Hawkish:**
The Federal Open Market Committee held the federal funds rate steady at **3.50%–3.75%** at its June 16–17 meeting — the fourth consecutive hold. The decision came under new Fed Chair **Kevin Warsh**, who emphasized an unwavering commitment to price stability. Critically, the latest FOMC dot plot shifted hawkish: **9 of 19 officials now see at least one rate hike in 2026**, and 6 see at least two. The median year-end funds rate projection moved to **3.8%**, pointing to a likely hike before December.

**PCE Inflation (May 2026 — Released Today):**
- Headline PCE: **+4.1% year-over-year** — highest since April 2023, and above April's 3.8%
- Core PCE: **+3.3% year-over-year** — unchanged from April
- Monthly: Headline +0.5% MoM; Core +0.37% MoM
- Interpretation: Inflation re-acceleration is driven significantly by energy price pass-through from the Iran conflict and Strait of Hormuz disruption. Economists broadly expect May to represent a near-peak reading, but the Fed cannot afford to be wrong. Rate cuts in 2026 are off the table; a hike by Q4 remains the base case of over half the FOMC.

**Key Data to Watch This Week:**
- June 25: May PCE (today — the centerpiece print)
- June 26: University of Michigan Consumer Sentiment (final June read)
- July 2: June Non-Farm Payrolls — next major macro event

## Geopolitical Factors

**Iran War & Strait of Hormuz (Critical — Ongoing):**
The 2026 Iran conflict has produced what the International Energy Agency has formally characterized as the **"largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market."** The closure of the Strait of Hormuz — through which roughly 20% of global oil trade flows — drove Brent crude up approximately 15% to **$83/barrel** (as of early March; prices remain elevated). U.S. gasoline prices rose 7.5% to ~$3.20/gallon in that initial shock wave. The conflict has also knocked **one-quarter of global LNG export capacity offline**, including damage to Qatar's Ras Laffan facility (roughly 20% of global LNG output) and continued Ukrainian strikes on Russian LNG infrastructure.

**Tariff & Trade Regime Uncertainty:**
A **U.S. Supreme Court ruling against IEEPA-based tariffs** reshuffled the trade policy landscape, constraining the executive branch's ability to unilaterally impose tariffs. This removes some inflation risk but introduces political uncertainty as the administration seeks legislative alternatives. The global economy remains in a fragmented, multipolar trade environment with significant barriers across all major blocs.

**Global Growth Impact:**
IMF reference forecasts hold global growth at just **3.1% in 2026** with headline global inflation at **4.4%** — a sharp deviation from the disinflation trend that characterized 2024–2025. Emerging markets exposed to energy imports face the most acute pressure.

## Sector Deep Dives

### Artificial Intelligence

The AI infrastructure spending cycle shows no signs of exhaustion in absolute capex terms, but margin stress is emerging among the hyperscalers. The four largest AI investors — **Microsoft, Alphabet, Amazon, and Meta** — are collectively targeting **$725 billion in capex in 2026**, up a staggering **77% from 2025's already record $410 billion**. However, Morgan Stanley estimates hyperscaler borrowing will exceed **$400 billion this year** — more than double 2025's $165 billion — as AI investment is outstripping organic cash generation. Amazon's Q1 capex of $44 billion outpaced operating cash flow of $26 billion. Oracle has crossed into negative free cash flow. Alphabet approaches that line around Q1 2027.

**Today's catalyst: Micron (MU)** — The AI memory trade is alive and well. Micron's Q3 FY2026 results were historic by any measure (see Hardware section). After-hours reaction: MU shares surged ~15%, lifting NVIDIA, AMD, TSMC, and the broader semiconductor complex in pre-market trading.

**Meta Platforms (META)** — Shares dropped ~7% on reports of a major share offering to fund ongoing AI buildout. The company has identified potential to cut Reality Labs spending by 30% (~$56 billion freed for AI redeployment), but the dilution overhang weighed on sentiment. Reality Labs has absorbed over $90 billion in cumulative losses since the segment was spun out.

**NVIDIA (NVDA)** — Down roughly 8% over the trailing month despite a blowout Q1 earnings report. The market's concern: if hyperscalers hit financing limits and trim orders, NVIDIA's demand curve bends with them. But Micron's $100 billion annualized data center revenue run rate and 16 locked-in multi-year supply agreements suggest the AI hardware cycle is durable, not a one-quarter wonder.

**Key Risk:** Memory is cyclical — if AI capex contracts sharply (hyperscaler financing constraints), pricing could reverse fast

**Rating: Strong Buy** (on any pullback from the +15% gap-open)

## Risk Factors

Ranked by severity for the week of June 23–27, 2026:

1. **PCE Inflation Surprise (HIGH — Today's Print):** May PCE at 4.1% is the highest since April 2023. If June follows suit, the Fed may move up the timeline for a rate hike. A 25 bps hike in September or November is increasingly baked into front-end rates, but an August emergency meeting scenario — while unlikely — cannot be fully dismissed if energy prices spike further.

2. **Strait of Hormuz — Escalation Risk (HIGH — Ongoing):** Any further military escalation in the Iran conflict that deepens the Hormuz closure beyond current damage assessments could push Brent crude through $100/barrel and add another 1–2 percentage points to headline PCE. This is the single biggest tail risk to both the economy and equity markets.

3. **Hyperscaler Free Cash Flow Cliff (MEDIUM-HIGH):** The math is sobering — Amazon, Oracle, and Meta are already at or through zero free cash flow when capex is included. If credit markets tighten (e.g., investment-grade spreads widen on inflation fears), hyperscalers facing $400 billion in annual borrowing requirements could be forced to trim AI orders. This is the #1 risk to the NVIDIA demand thesis.

4. **Fed Policy Error — Too Hawkish (MEDIUM):** With 9 FOMC members now projecting rate hikes, markets are at risk of a sharp reprice in rate-sensitive equities (utilities, REITs, long-duration growth) if the dot plot moves further hawkish. Chair Warsh's "price stability above all" stance introduces more hawkish variance than the prior Powell regime.

5. **Earnings Season Guidance Risk (MEDIUM):** Q2 earnings season begins in mid-July. Several large-cap tech companies guided conservatively in Q1 amid macro uncertainty. If forward guidance disappoints in the wake of strong Q1 prints, the P/E re-ratings of the past six months could partially reverse.

## Bottom Line

Today is a risk-on session driven by one of the most compelling earnings prints in semiconductor history — Micron's AI memory results confirm the infrastructure buildout is not slowing and that pricing power in advanced memory has structurally shifted upward. However, the May PCE print at 4.1% is an uncomfortable simultaneous reminder that the monetary environment has tightened materially, and that the Fed's next move is more likely up than down. For disciplined investors: lean into high-conviction AI infrastructure (MU, TSM) and undervalued software (GWRE, FICO) while keeping position sizes measured against the real and ongoing risk of a geopolitical energy shock that could force the Fed's hand.
