# Daily Market Report — July 23, 2026

## Executive Summary
Markets enter Thursday's session digesting a mixed Wednesday close, a jump in oil prices tied to escalating Strait of Hormuz tensions, and a wave of Big Tech earnings reactions (Alphabet capex guidance spooked investors, Tesla missed estimates, IBM trimmed its sales outlook). Speculative risk appetite remains intact in micro-cap biotech even as mega-cap semis trade flat, underscoring a market that is rotating rather than retreating.

## Macro & Monetary Environment
The Fed holds its benchmark rate at 3.50%–3.75% heading into the July 29 FOMC decision — the meeting is now a genuine swing event rather than a formality. Inflation projections for 2026 have been revised up to roughly 3.6%, and under Chair Kevin Warsh the Fed's rhetoric has turned more hawkish, with markets assigning roughly a 25% probability to a surprise 25bp hike rather than a cut. That repricing is a meaningful shift from the "gradual easing" narrative that dominated markets earlier this year, and it is contributing to the VIX's recent volatility even as the index sits at a still-low 17.05. This week's key watch items: the July 29 rate decision itself, continued Q2 earnings from mega-cap tech and industrials, and any fresh core PCE or CPI commentary ahead of the meeting.

## Geopolitical Factors
The Strait of Hormuz remains the single largest geopolitical tail risk to markets. Renewed US-Iran hostilities have sharply reduced vessel traffic through the waterway — from an average of roughly 100 ships to single digits — raising insurance costs and the probability of intermittent supply disruption in a corridor that carries about 20% of global oil flows and nearly 20% of global LNG trade. Brent crude spiked roughly 3.4% to $94.07/bbl and WTI rose about 3% to $86.83/bbl in the latest session on this news. Secondary risk factors include lingering Red Sea shipping disruptions, Panama Canal restrictions, and persistent uncertainty around Taiwan. A prolonged Hormuz disruption could push Brent toward $100/bbl, which would complicate the Fed's rate path and add volatility across risk assets heading into month-end.

## Sector Deep Dives

### Today's Spotlight: Semiconductors & Semicap Equipment
The chip complex is showing a widening valuation gap between AI-infrastructure darlings and the broader analog/equipment/packaging supply chain. Mega-cap AI silicon names were roughly flat this week even as regional Asian chipmakers rallied on continued AI buildout optimism, suggesting rotation within the sector rather than a broad re-rating. Beneath the surface, three distinct sub-stories stand out: (1) **advanced packaging/OSAT** capacity (Amkor, and the broader CoWoS/HBM ecosystem) is arguably the most under-priced link in the AI hardware chain, trading at a fraction of the multiple assigned to front-end logic and equipment names; (2) **analog/power semiconductors** (onsemi and peers) are in the early stages of a cyclical recovery, with AI data-center power content roughly doubling in 2026 even as legacy auto/industrial demand remains soft; and (3) **semicap equipment** valuations have become bifurcated — process-control and inspection names (Camtek, Onto) trade at rich PEG multiples on HBM4 optimism, while photomask suppliers (Photronics) trade at a persistent discount to the group despite a structurally protected duopoly position. Screening for names with reasonable PEG ratios and defensible moats outside the most crowded AI-equipment trade — memory/interconnect IP licensors, packaging specialists, and photonics suppliers to AI data centers — surfaced several names with better risk/reward than the most-owned front-end equipment stocks.

### Artificial Intelligence
Alphabet's Q2 print was the marquee AI event of the week: cloud revenue beat expectations meaningfully (one analyst called it "a massive beat"), but shares fell nearly 5% in extended trading after management raised its full-year capex forecast, reviving investor anxiety about the AI infrastructure spending cycle's payback period. That tension — strong underlying demand signals versus rising capital intensity — is now the central debate for every hyperscaler reporting this earnings season. Regional Asian chipmakers rallied on the same AI-buildout thesis that is pressuring hyperscaler margins, a reminder that capex anxiety at the customer level often shows up as bullish read-through for the supply chain.

### Technology (Broad)
Big Tech earnings dominate the tape this week. Tesla missed earnings estimates and IBM lowered its annual sales outlook, adding to a mixed picture for large-cap tech even as cloud and AI infrastructure names outperform. Broader software/internet trading has taken a back seat to the hardware and capex story, with investors more focused on capital allocation discipline than top-line growth alone.

### Hardware & Semiconductors
Oil-driven cost inflation and capex-guidance jitters have made this a stock-picker's week in hardware. Regional chipmakers in Asia outperformed on AI-buildout optimism, while the highest-multiple U.S. AI-infrastructure connectivity names have started to see some multiple compression as investors reassess how much growth is already priced in. Equipment and materials suppliers tied to leading-edge and HBM capacity remain the most closely watched sub-group heading into the back half of the year.

### Software & SaaS
Enterprise software was relatively quiet this week outside of the earnings calendar, with IBM's guidance cut serving as the notable negative data point. Analyst commentary this week (Salesforce, ServiceNow among names mentioned in top research notes) continues to center on AI-monetization progress within existing enterprise software franchises rather than new franchise creation — a theme likely to persist through the rest of earnings season.

## Stock Picks — Valuation-Based

### 1. AMKR — Amkor Technology, Inc.
**Thesis:** The leading independent OSAT (outsourced assembly and test) provider is arguably the cheapest way to buy into the AI advanced-packaging boom, with management guiding for its AI packaging portfolio to triple in 2026 and a new Arizona facility ramping in the back half of the year.
**Valuation Metrics:** P/E (trailing) ~35–49x (source-dependent); P/S (forward) ~2.35x; the stock trades at a discount to the broader semiconductor industry average multiple despite AI-driven growth.
**Growth Profile:** Revenue +27.5% YoY (TTM); 2026 consensus EPS of $2.08, +38.7% YoY.
**Competitive Moat:** Scale and process know-how in advanced packaging (CoWoS-adjacent, fan-out, 2.5D/3D) that is difficult and capital-intensive to replicate; deep, sticky relationships with fabless AI chip customers.
**Key Risk:** Thesis leans heavily on CPU advanced-packaging mass production beginning in H2 2026 — delays there would push out the re-rating catalyst.
**Rating:** Buy

### 2. RMBS — Rambus Inc.
**Thesis:** A high-margin, capital-light licensor of memory-interface and interconnect IP that is a direct, diversified beneficiary of HBM4/DDR5 and chiplet adoption without single-customer concentration risk.
**Valuation Metrics:** Forward P/E ~33x; EV/EBITDA ~31x; PEG in-line with growth given a royalty-based, high-incremental-margin model.
**Growth Profile:** 2025 revenue +27% YoY to $707.6M; consensus forecasts ~15% annual revenue growth over the next three years.
**Competitive Moat:** Deep patent portfolio and standards-body position in memory-interface technology; royalty stream is largely decoupled from any single chip design win.
**Key Risk:** Stock has already round-tripped a large post-earnings move this year (surged then gave back gains after Q1 2026 results), signaling sensitivity to any growth deceleration.
**Rating:** Buy

### 3. PLAB — Photronics, Inc.
**Thesis:** The market's most overlooked semicap-adjacent value name — a photomask duopoly supplier (alongside Toppan) trading at roughly half the sector's forward multiple while investing in advanced photomask capacity for AI-driven demand.
**Valuation Metrics:** Forward P/E ~18x (vs. ~36x semiconductor industry median); trailing P/E ~12x.
**Growth Profile:** Revenue roughly flat near-term (Q2 FY26 revenue ~$210M, essentially flat YoY); modest ~1% projected revenue growth next fiscal year, with Flat Panel Display segment outperforming.
**Competitive Moat:** High capital intensity and long qualification cycles create a durable two-player global structure in photomasks; sticky, multi-year customer relationships.
**Key Risk:** Growth is genuinely slow — this is a valuation/turnaround story, not a growth story, and a prolonged industry downturn would test the "cheap for a reason" thesis.
**Rating:** Accumulate

### 4. ON — onsemi (ON Semiconductor Corporation)
**Thesis:** A cyclical analog/power-semiconductor recovery story trading at a discount to its industry despite AI data-center revenue set to double in 2026, with earnings inflecting sharply off a cyclical trough.
**Valuation Metrics:** Forward P/E ~36x; trades at a modest discount to the Semiconductor – Analog & Mixed industry group.
**Growth Profile:** Revenue currently down -9% YoY (cyclical trough) but 2026 consensus EPS of $3.09 implies +31.5% growth, with 2027 EPS of $4.36 implying +41.1% further growth.
**Competitive Moat:** Leading silicon-carbide (SiC) power semiconductor position serving EV, industrial, and now AI data-center power delivery; long-standing automotive/industrial design-in relationships.
**Key Risk:** Auto and industrial end-markets remain soft; the recovery thesis depends on AI data-center power demand offsetting continued legacy weakness.
**Rating:** Buy

### 5. COHR — Coherent Corp.
**Thesis:** The dominant independent supplier of lasers, optical transceivers, and photonic components moving data inside and between AI data centers — a growth-at-a-reasonable-price way to play AI infrastructure build-out beyond compute silicon itself.
**Valuation Metrics:** Forward P/E ~48.6x (trailing ~151x, reflecting a low prior-year earnings base); PEG well below 1x on forward growth estimates.
**Growth Profile:** Revenue $1.8B, +21% YoY; forecast to grow revenue ~20% and earnings ~52–54% annually; Datacenter & Communications segment (~75% of revenue) grew over 40% YoY.
**Competitive Moat:** Vertically integrated optics/photonics manufacturing scale and a multi-year technology lead in high-speed optical interconnect, a bottleneck component for AI data-center scale-up.
**Key Risk:** At ~$77B market cap and a rich forward multiple even after adjustment, the stock has limited room for any growth disappointment; a slowdown in hyperscaler optical-interconnect orders would hit the multiple hard.
**Rating:** Accumulate

## Risk Factors
1. **Fed policy surprise (High):** A hawkish surprise or even a hold with hawkish guidance at the July 29 meeting, against a backdrop of inflation revised up to 3.6%, could trigger a sharp repricing in rate-sensitive growth stocks.
2. **Strait of Hormuz escalation (High):** Vessel traffic has collapsed to single digits from a ~100-ship average; a sustained disruption could push Brent toward $100/bbl, reigniting inflation and complicating the Fed's path.
3. **AI capex payback anxiety (Medium-High):** Alphabet's post-earnings drop on raised capex guidance shows investor patience for hyperscaler AI spending is thinning; further capex raises without commensurate revenue proof could pressure the entire AI supply chain.
4. **Earnings season dispersion (Medium):** Tesla's miss and IBM's guidance cut show this earnings season is rewarding/punishing execution more than sector membership — idiosyncratic single-stock risk is elevated into month-end.
5. **Small-cap/large-cap divergence (Medium):** Russell 2000's +19.3% YTD versus S&P's +9.5% signals a broadening rally, but small-caps are also more rate-sensitive — a hawkish Fed surprise could hit this outperformance hardest.

## Bottom Line
This is a market rotating under the surface rather than trending cleanly in either direction: mega-cap AI capex anxiety, a genuinely live Fed decision, and a real geopolitical oil shock are all in play simultaneously. Within semiconductors, the better risk-adjusted opportunities right now look less like the most-owned AI-equipment names and more like the packaging, IP-licensing, and analog-recovery stories the market has been slower to re-rate. Stay selective, size positions for a binary Fed outcome on July 29, and watch Hormuz shipping data as the single highest-impact swing factor for the week.
