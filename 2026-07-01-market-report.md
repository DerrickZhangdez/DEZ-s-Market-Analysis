## Sector Deep Dives

### Today's Spotlight: AI Infrastructure & Data Centers

*Day-of-year 182 → bucket 2: AI infrastructure & data centers (power, cooling, networking, interconnect, memory). This is the deepest section of today's report.*

#### The Big Picture: The Bottleneck Has Moved

Twelve months ago, the AI buildout constraint was silicon: GPUs, HBM memory, and CoWoS advanced packaging capacity. Today, those constraints have partially eased (though TSMC's CoWoS remains backlogged). The new bottleneck is **energy and thermal management**:

1. **Grid interconnection queues** in the U.S., Europe, and Asia are measured in years. A new hyperscale data center requesting grid connection in Northern Virginia or Texas faces a 2–4 year queue in many markets, with utilities requiring hundreds of millions in upfront infrastructure investment.
2. **Thermal management** has become mission-critical. Liquid cooling holds heat approximately 3,000 times more effectively than air — and AI clusters running NVL72/NVL144 GPU configurations at 130kW+ per rack *require* liquid cooling solutions. The forced migration from air to liquid cooling is now irreversible for AI-class hardware.
3. **Power delivery** to GPUs is increasingly sophisticated — high-density intelligent PDUs, busbar systems, and UPS configurations must handle power densities 3–5× higher than traditional data centers.

#### Power and Energy: Nuclear's AI Moment

The AI power demand signal is driving a renaissance in nuclear power, baseload natural gas, and utility-scale renewables:

- **Constellation Energy (CEG)** signed a landmark 20-year power purchase agreement with Microsoft to restart **Three Mile Island Unit 1**, providing carbon-free baseload power for Microsoft's AI data centers — a watershed event that validated nuclear power as the preferred long-duration power source for hyperscalers.
- **Data center power demand** is projected to require 35–45 GW of new U.S. power generation by 2030, according to the IEA — roughly equivalent to adding 35–45 nuclear reactors' worth of generating capacity.
- The **grid interconnection queue crisis** is structurally the most underappreciated bottleneck in the AI infrastructure buildout. This creates compelling investment cases in power transmission, electrical contracting, and baseload generation.

#### Thermal Management & Cooling: A Trillion-Dollar Plumbing Problem

The liquid cooling market for data centers was valued at approximately $6 billion in 2026 and is projected to reach $27 billion by 2035 (18.2% CAGR), driven entirely by AI GPU density increases:

- **Modine Manufacturing (MOD)** emerged as the standout in fiscal 2026 with data center revenue surging **73% to $1.1 billion** — the thermal management division outgrew the parent company dramatically. In a landmark transaction announced during FY2026, Modine secured a **$4+ billion long-term agreement** to supply data center cooling products for calendar years 2027–2029. This contract alone provides extraordinary revenue visibility and validates Modine's transition from an automotive-first company to a data center infrastructure pure-play.
- **nVent Electric (NVT)** reported Q1 2026 revenues up 53% YoY, with its Infrastructure segment (gray/white space data center products) growing **more than 100% organically**. Organic orders were up approximately 65% in recent quarters. nVent's product portfolio includes liquid-cooling distribution units, high-density intelligent power distribution units, and enclosures purpose-built for AI-dense rack configurations.
- **Vertiv Holdings (VRT)** remains the best-known name in the space — its revenue backlog recently reached $15 billion — but has already surged dramatically and trades at elevated multiples that limit near-term upside for new positions.

#### Connectivity Silicon & Networking

The interconnect stack between GPUs and memory is the nervous system of AI clusters:

- **Astera Labs (ALAB)** is the standout interconnect growth story: Q1 2026 revenue reached $308.4 million, up 93% YoY, with net income of $80.3 million (+152%). The Scorpio X-Series 320-lane Smart Fabric Switch is shipping into a merchant scale-up silicon market projected to reach $20 billion by 2030. However, ALAB's valuation (P/E ~197x, EV/EBITDA ~301x, forward P/S ~25x) reflects extreme growth expectations — it is categorically not a value pick, but a legitimate AI infrastructure benchmark.
- **Arista Networks (ANET)** provides high-speed Ethernet switches purpose-built for AI clusters. Networking represents roughly 10–15% of a cluster's total cost but is mission-critical. Arista's Ethernet-for-AI strategy has gained significant traction as an alternative to InfiniBand.
- **Marvell Technology** has captured the new wave of custom ASIC designs from AWS and Microsoft and completed its acquisition of Celestial AI (optical interconnects) in February 2026.

#### Memory and Advanced Packaging

- **HBM (High-Bandwidth Memory):** AI GPU deployments continue to sustain extraordinary HBM demand. SK Hynix and Micron remain the dominant HBM suppliers, with pricing firm as supply has not fully caught up with demand.
- **Advanced Packaging:** TSMC's CoWoS (Chip-on-Wafer-on-Substrate) remains the capacity gating factor for the highest-end GPU assemblies. Amkor Technology (AMKR), Powertech Technology, and ASE Group are growing as alternative packaging capacity comes online.
- **Thermal Interface Materials:** Indium Corporation, Parker Hannifin, and Henkel provide specialized TIMs that manage heat transfer between chips and cooling systems — a small but structurally important market.

#### Investment Thesis for AI Infrastructure Today

The key insight: **AI infrastructure is no longer a semiconductor story alone.** As GPU supply normalized (relatively), the next multi-year constraint is power, cooling, and connectivity. Companies operating in those segments with defensible market positions, contractual revenue visibility, and mid-cap valuations offer a compelling risk/reward — particularly those that have not yet received the same analyst coverage as Vertiv or Arista.
